Join us at our first 24 hour hackathon in Reading!

R. U. Hacking? is a 24 hour student hackathon at the University of Reading. Spring 2018 will be the first 24 hour hackathon by the society, with prior 6 hour taster sessions running over the previous two academic years.

The hackathon will run across the weekend which offers students the chance to split into groups, brainstorm project ideas, and create a working prototype before the time limit is up! On Sunday afternoon teams will present their work and prizes will be awarded based on the commitee's decisions.

Whether it's your first hackathon, or your 50th, we'd love to see students of all skill levels and from all universities come along for a weekend of creativity, collaboration and code.

Hacka-what?

As BrumHack best describes it, it's an "invention marathon".
Amongst the computing community, to hack is to build in as short a time as possible with no thought given to long-term maintenance, simply to demonstrate an idea or play around with a new technology.

What you'll need

Whether you're a grizzled veteran in coding or just a beginner, all you'll need at the event is a good mood. Oh, a sleeping bag and a pillow also help if you're planning on sleeping during the event!
There will be plenty of people to network with during the event, so you can arrange a team up to 6 people before or during the event!

Welcome to Reading!

Situated at the heart of Thames Valley, the University of Reading has close ties to industry thanks to it being close to the M4. This is known as the “Silicon Valley” of the UK due to the vast number of technology companies that have setup their headquarters here.
The University is very invested in innovation, homing the Thames Valley Science Park, home to Reading Enterprise Centre and the Science & Technology Centre.

And best of all - it's all free!

Thanks to our amazing sponsors, students don't have to spend anything to attend!
Better yet, some will be attending the event offering help, hosting workshops and potentially recruiting!
